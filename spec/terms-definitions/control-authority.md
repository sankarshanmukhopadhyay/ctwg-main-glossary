[[def: control-authority, control-authority, control authority]]

~ In identity systems, control authority is the power to determine who controls what. It is a primary factor in determining the basis for trust in those systems.

~ The entity with *control authority* takes action through operations that affect the:
~ - creation (inception)
~ - updating
~ - rotation
~ - revocation
~ - deletion
~ - delegation
~ of authentication factors and their relation to the identifier.

~ Note: Control authority is especially important because it identifies where effective operational power resides, including the power to change, invalidate, or reassign control relationships.
