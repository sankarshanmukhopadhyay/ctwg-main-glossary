# Term Quality Rubric

Use this rubric when creating or refining glossary terms.

| Check | Question |
|---|---|
| Clarity | Is the term definition precise and readable without circularity? |
| Scope | Does the definition make clear what is in scope and out of scope? |
| Operational relevance | Would an implementer understand where this term matters in a real system? |
| Authority | If the term involves decision rights, is the actor and authority boundary clear? |
| Delegation | If delegation is possible, is that stated and bounded? |
| Lifecycle | Are issuance, update, expiry, suspension, revocation, or replacement relevant and described? |
| Evidence | Does the term imply logs, attestations, proofs, or records worth naming? |
| Cross-reference quality | Are related terms linked cleanly and usefully? |
| Machine-readability path | Would this term benefit from structured metadata or export later? |
| Source quality | Are references authoritative and current enough for glossary use? |

## Prioritization rule

Prioritize governance-heavy terms first. These are terms that shape who may act, who may decide, who may revoke, and what evidence can later be inspected.
